Study Visa

How to Study in Canada: The Complete Guide

Understanding Study Permits

Studying in Canada offers international students world-class education and a pathway to permanent residency. Here's what you need to know:

Eligibility Requirements:
  • Acceptance letter from a designated learning institution
  • Proof of sufficient funds for 12 months of living expenses
  • Clean criminal record
  • Medical examination results
Expected Costs:
  • Tuition: $8,000 - $35,000 CAD/year
  • Living expenses: $10,000 - $15,000 CAD/year
  • Health insurance: $500 - $1,000 CAD/year
Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP):

After graduation, international students are eligible for a Post-Graduation Work Permit, allowing 2-3 years of Canadian work experience. This is a crucial step toward permanent residency through the Canadian Experience Class (CEC).

Typical Salaries for International Students:
  • On-campus jobs: $15-20 CAD/hour
  • Co-op work terms: $18-25 CAD/hour
  • Post-graduation: $40,000 - $60,000 CAD/year
Work Permit

Work Permits in Canada: Your Path to Living Wage

Types of Work Permits Available

Canada offers several types of work permits based on your qualifications and job offer:

1. Closed Work Permit
  • Job offer required
  • Employer-specific
  • Typical salaries: $40,000 - $70,000 CAD/year
2. Open Work Permit
  • No job offer needed initially
  • Spouse of international students
  • Typical salaries: $35,000 - $60,000 CAD/year
3. Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP)
  • For international graduates
  • Valid for 1-3 years
  • Typical salaries: $45,000 - $75,000 CAD/year
Processing Times & Costs:
  • Standard processing: 2-8 weeks
  • Express processing: 24 hours (additional fee applies)
  • Application fee: $330 CAD
